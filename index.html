It's Hurricane Season

Updated: 12/12/2020 245 Likes

Are you prepared?

Even though the Atlantic Hurricane Season only officially began on June 1, Dominion Energy has been preparing for months.

This article is about this:

How to know when a storm is coming

What is important to pack in an emergency preparedness kit

What to do when the power goes out

How to stay safe during and after the storm

Even though the Atlantic Hurricane Season only officially began on June 1, Dominion Energy has been preparing for months. "Summer storms can be unpredictable so planning ahead and being ready is key," explains David Vanderbloemen, director of the Electric Distribution Operations Center. "Part of our preparation includes using new technologies to restore power faster if it does go out," he explains. But there's a lot that you, the customer, can do to stay safe, too.

Watch the Weather

Know your evacuation zone. You can find it using a simple tool on the Department of Emergency Management's website . Follow the storm on the National Hurricane Center's website as it approaches.

Click here and sign up here to receive storm updates from Dominion Energy via text or email.

Pack an Emergency Kit

  • Download a list of what to pack in an emergency kit [here]. Don't wait for a hurricane watch or warning to prepare. By then, the shelves will be bare or stores will be closed.
  • Use large plastic totes, laundry baskets, or suitcases to organize everything

Prepare to Be Powerless

  • Go online or call 1-800-222-0401 to make sure we've got your correct phone number. (This will help if you have to report an outage).
  • Charge your cell phone, rechargeable batteries, iPads, and laptop before the storm hits.
  • We don't know you've lost power unless you tell us. Report your outage here or call us at 866-366-4357.
  • Leave your front porch light on so that if you have to ask someone to drive by and check on the house they can easily tell of you have power.
  • Only use generators outdoors and with plenty of ventilation. Never plug a generator into a wall socket, which could cause an explosion.
  • Use our outage map to stay up to date on restoration efforts.
  • If you're without power, turn off major appliances, such as air conditioners, water heaters, and stoves.
  • Unplug TVs, stereos, microwaves, and computers to prevent damage when the power is restored.
  • Fill up your car with gas.

Stay Safe in the Storm

  • If you're told to evacuate, do so immediately
  • If you see a downed power line, stay back 30 feet and remove children and pets from the area. (Remember, just because a line isn't moving or sparking, doesn't mean it isn't dangerous).
  • Call us immediately at 866-366-4357 to report downed power lines.
  • Don't drive through standing water.
  • If you are in a car and a power line falls on it, please stay in your car and call 911 immediately. The rubber wheels on your vehicle will keep you safe inside, but the metal exterior and ground around your car could be energized. Click here for a video with more advice.

Emergency Kit Packing List

  • At least 3 days of non-perishable food for each person
  • 1 gallon of water per person, per day
  • Can opener
  • First Aid kit
  • 1 blanket or sleeping bag per person
  • 1 change of clothes per person
  • Flashlight and lantern
  • Battery-operated weather radio
  • Extra batteries
  • Prescription and over-the-counter medications
  • Food and water for pet(s)
  • Important family documents (IDs, bank records, passports, insurance policies)
  • Matches or lighter
  • Cash
  • Whistle to signal for help
  • Wet wipes for washing up
